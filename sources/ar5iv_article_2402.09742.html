Abstract

The incorporation of Large Language Models (LLMs) in healthcare marks a significant advancement. However, the application has predominantly been limited to discriminative and question-answering tasks, which does not fully leverage their interactive potential. To address this limitation, our paper presents AI Hospital, a framework designed to build a real-time interactive diagnosis environment. To simulate the procedure, we collect high-quality medical records to create patient, examiner, and medical director agents. AI Hospital is then utilized for the interactive evaluation and collaboration of LLMs. Initially, we create a Multi-View Medical Evaluation (MVME) benchmark where various LLMs serve as intern doctors for interactive diagnosis. Subsequently, to improve diagnostic accuracy, we introduce a collaborative mechanism that involves iterative discussions and a dispute resolution process under the supervision of the medical director. In our experiments, we validate the reliability of AI Hospital. The results not only explore the feasibility of apply LLMs in clinical consultation but also confirm the effectiveness of the dispute resolution focused collaboration method. 1 1 1 https://github.com/LibertFan/AI_Hospital

1 Introduction

The realm of healthcare has witnessed a remarkable evolution with the integration of artificial intelligence Bajwa et¬†al. ( 2021 ) , particularly through the development of Large Language Models (LLMs) OpenAI ( 2023 ); Touvron et¬†al. ( 2023 ); Bai et¬†al. ( 2023 ) . These advancements open novel pathways for medicine Singhal et¬†al. ( 2023 ); Chen et¬†al. ( 2023e ); Bao et¬†al. ( 2023 ); Zhang et¬†al. ( 2023 ) , with LLMs demonstrating remarkable performance across various medical question-answering benchmarks Jin et¬†al. ( 2021 , 2019 ); Pal et¬†al. ( 2022 ) . Nonetheless, the complexity of real-world medical consultations, which typically involve a series of steps such as assessing patient conditions, performing physical and auxiliary examinations, and offering medication and health guidance, presents a challenge. These steps require intricate, multi-turn interactions with patients, raising questions about the efficacy of LLMs in managing real-world medical consultations.

In this work, we introduce AI Hospital, a platform based on multi-agent interaction Park et¬†al. ( 2023 ); Li et¬†al. ( 2023 ) to build a real-time interactive consultation scenario, and explore the feasibility of using LLMs as doctors in clinical diagnosis. The platform incorporates various roles including intern doctors, patients, examiners, and a medical director. In the framework, intern doctors diagnose diseases through active questioning, patients need to describe their symptoms and respond to inquiries from doctors, examiners execute medical tests following the doctors‚Äô instructions, while the medical director is tasked with assessing the performance of the intern doctors in diagnosis. Significantly, the primary objective of AI Hospital is twofold: firstly, facilitating interactive consultation among intern doctors, patients and examiners to evaluate the diagnostic capability of LLMs , and secondly, enabling collaborative efforts among doctors , thereby improving the diagnostic accuracy.

Establishing AI Hospital necessitates overcoming the challenge of replicating the behavior of personnel in medical settings, such as patients and examiners. (1) To address the issue of medical roles lacking medical knowledge, we collect high-quality Chinese medical records, utilizing professional medical data as inputs for large language models to effectively simulate the agents. (2) Given the critical role of professional medical examinations in clinical diagnosis, with CDC finding that 70% medical decisions rely on them di¬†Ruffano et¬†al. ( 2012 ); CDC ( 2018 ) , we establish an examiner role specifically for recording and overseeing patients‚Äô physical and auxiliary examination results. This configuration also restricts patient agents from accessing detailed medical examination data, ensuring the alignment in behavior with actual patients. (3) To mitigate patients‚Äô lack of self-awareness in conversation scenarios Wang et¬†al. ( 2023c ) , we craft background narratives, personas and descriptions of conditions with personal characteristics to guide the conversational style. In summary, we intricately craft the prompts for these medical roles, allowing for natural interaction with intern doctors grounding on the setting of role.

Based on the AI Hospital, we investigate the feasibility of utilizing LLMs as intern doctors for clinical diagnosis by establishing a Multi-View Medical Evaluation (MVME) benchmark. The benchmark assesses the real-time interactive consultation capabilities of LLMs across three critical dimensions. Firstly, it examines their ability to identify patient symptoms, highlighting the importance of actively seeking relevant information. Secondly, we assess the comprehensiveness of their medical examinations, specifically their adeptness in selecting and administering a suitable range of these tests. Thirdly, we measure the accuracy and professionalism of their diagnosis, checking whether they meet standards of medical practice. In practice, we not only leverage GPT-4 as a flexible evaluation engine but also employ a link-based method to incorporate medical standard knowledge.

To enhance LLMs‚Äô diagnostic accuracy, drawing on previous research Croft et¬†al. ( 2015 ); Llewelyn et¬†al. ( 2014 ); Centor et¬†al. ( 2019 ) that highlights the importance of teamwork in clinical diagnosis, we delve into the collaborative mechanism O‚Äôleary et¬†al. ( 2010 ); Lamb et¬†al. ( 2011 ) , facilitating iterative discussions among intern doctors. Different from previous models that lack concise organization and clear direction for participants Talebirad and Nadiri ( 2023 ); Liang et¬†al. ( 2023 ) , our framework introduces a dispute resolution strategy that effectively engages the medical director to guide discussions, clarify issues, and steer the interns towards a more structured and efficient collaboration, thereby fostering more focused discussions and accelerating the achievement of consensus.

In our experiment, we initially investigate the behavior of various personnel within the AI Hospital to validate the reliability. Subsequently, we test a range of LLMs in the AI Hospital, uncovering that deficiencies in interaction significantly impair diagnostic accuracy. Furthermore, we assess the effectiveness of our dispute resolution-focused cooperative mechanism, demonstrating its capacity to accelerate and enhance diagnostic accuracy. Finally, we dive into GPT-4, analyzing its limited efficacy in identifying necessary medical examinations and managing complications.

In general, our contributions are of three-folds:

  • ‚Ä¢

    We develop AI Hospital underpinned by an LLM-driven multi-agent framework.

  • ‚Ä¢

    We build a benchmark MVME for interactive evaluation within the medical domain to assess LLMs in interactive consultations.

  • ‚Ä¢

    We design a collaborative mechanism which focuses on dispute resolution. It demonstrates diagnostic capabilities surpassing GPT-4.

2 Setup of AI Hospital

The framework of our AI Hospital is illustrated in Figure 1 . It encompasses three principal procedures: (1) Intern doctors proactively question patients about their physical condition and patients obtain diagnostic reports from intern doctors; (2) Patients undertake medical examinations following the directions of intern doctors; (3) Medical director assess the performance of intern doctors. In this section, ¬ß 2.1 details how we utilize the collected medical records to equip medical roles with necessary medical expertise. Subsequently, ¬ß 2.2 to ¬ß 2.5 describe the methodologies employed to establish the four agents, including the intern doctor.

2.1 Agents Setup on Medical Records

We collect a substantial volume of Chinese medical record data, comprising various sections. Given the requirement of specific medical knowledge in different roles, we categorize the information of medical record into three types.
Basic Information. It is consisting of chief complaints, current and past medical histories, and personal and familial histories.
Professional Medical Examination. It encompasses physical and auxiliary examinations.
Diagnosis and Treatment. It has diagnostic results, diagnostic rationales, and treatment course.
These categories are correspondingly allocated to the patient, examiner, and medical director, then employed in prompts of LLMs to facilitate the medical knowledge of distinct roles.

2.2 Intern Doctor

In AI Hospital, intern doctors are responsible for clinical diagnosis. This requires them to actively engage with patients, delving into detailed inquiries about their physical conditions. Through these interactions, intern doctors are expected to apply their medical knowledge and skills to identify potential illnesses afflicting the patients. Subsequently, the doctors conduct medical examinations through examiners to carry out a thorough investigation. Once a diagnosis is identified, the intern doctors then move to the formulation of treatment plans. They are expected to consider various treatment options, weigh their effectiveness, and select the most appropriate course of action. The prompt of intern doctor is shown in Table 12 .

2.3 Examiner

It is designed to manage ‚ÄúProfessional Medical Examination‚Äù as detailed in ¬ß 2.1 . 2.4 Patient

Functionality. We conduct a systematic examination of the essential capabilities required from patients during interactive diagnosis. These capabilities include: (1) the articulate expression of complaints; (2) truthful responses to doctors‚Äô inquiries; (3) active participation in examinations; and (4) the voicing of questions and concerns. We tailor prompts accordingly to meet the requirements. The prompt is shown in Table 9 .
Personality. To facilitate improved behavior control, we propose enhancing self-awareness in patient role-playing through the development of a patient‚Äôs personality. First, we utilize GPT-4 to reason and imagine the detailed aspects of the patient‚Äôs lifestyle, pertinent events, personality traits, and speaking manner that correlate with their physical condition. There is an example: ‚ÄúYou are a farmer who left school after primary education. Your life is frugal, but your mindset is optimistic. By crafting comprehensive patient profiles as prompts and employing one-shot learning, the patient agent can replicate the behaviors of a real patient in dialogues.

2.5 Medical Director

The primary responsibility of the medical director is to assess the performance of intern doctors in interactive diagnosis. At the end of a consultation, the medical director asks the intern doctors to provide a comprehensive summary that includes the patient‚Äôs symptom, medical examinations, diagnostic results, diagnostic rationales, and the treatment plan. This procedure is designed to consolidate information regarding the patient‚Äôs state, elucidate the interns‚Äô comprehension of the diagnosed condition, the basis of their judgments, and the treatment strategy. Upon receiving responses about these five areas, the medical director evaluates each section by comparing it with the medical record, which serves as the gold standard. 3 MVME: Evaluation of LLMs as Intern Doctors for Clinical Diagnosis

Based on AI Hospital, we assess the feasibility of employing LLMs for clinical diagnosis.

3.1 Multi-View Evaluation Criteria

We develop evaluation criteria from three aspects to assess the procedure of clinical diagnosis.
Mastery of Patient Symptoms. This assesses how precisely the intern doctors can gather and understand information about patient symptoms. It focuses on their ability to comprehensively explore the medical history, identify critical symptoms based on patient descriptions, and perform necessary analyses and documentation.
Comprehensiveness of Medical Examination. This evaluates the range and appropriateness of the medical tests employed by the interns. It considers their choice and skill in interpreting the findings.
Quality of Diagnostic Report. This measures the accuracy and professionalism of the diagnostic report, including the diagnostic results, diagnostic rationales, and the proposed treatment plan. It examines the report‚Äôs consistency with medical logic and standards.

Centering on three evaluation criteria, our assessment covers five specific aspects: (1) symptom, (2) medical examination, (3) diagnostic results, (4) diagnostic rationales, and (5) treatment plan.

3.2 Evaluation Methods

LLM based Evaluation with Reference

To assist the medical director in evaluation of the five specific portions, we extract the ‚ÄúDiagnosis and Treatment‚Äù with ‚ÄúChief Complaints‚Äù and ‚ÄúAuxiliary Examinations‚Äù from the medical records which are described in¬†¬ß 2.1 . The information is incorporated into the prompts for the LLMs (GPT-4) as Table 13 & 14 , providing external expertise for the medical director to assess LLMs. In each specific evaluation dimension, medical director rate the interns‚Äô performance on a scale of 1 to 4, ranging from poorest to excellent.

Link based Evaluation of Diagnostic Results

To enhance the scientific rigor and validity of our evaluations, particularly in diagnostic results with respect to identify diseases, we employ the International Classification of Diseases (ICD-10) Trott ( 1977 ) as the authoritative source, and link standardized disease terminologies with natural language based diagnostic results. Initially, we extract disease entities from the diagnostic results and the medical records‚Äô references. Subsequently, we implement a fuzzy matching process with a predefined threshold of 0.5 to link these disease entities with ICD-10 terminology, building a normalized disease sets. Ultimately, we assess diagnostic agreement between the diagnoses from intern doctors and the golden reference, then report the number of extracted disease entities (#), set-level precision (P), recall (R), and F1-score (F) metrics.

3.3 MVME Dataset Construction

We collect Chinese medical records across diverse departments online 2 2 2 https://bingli.iiyi.com/ and engage professional physicians for a thorough review. Subsequent to the exclusion of records with deficiencies, such as incomplete information, a total of 506 cases remained. The detailed distribution of these cases among the various departments is presented in Table 1 . To verify the quality of the collected medical records, we select samples from 10 secondary departments, randomly choosing five cases per department for review. Doctors from corresponding departments are hired to evaluate the ‚ÄúDiagnosis and Treatment‚Äù including diagnostic results, diagnostic rationales, and treatment course with a binary choice: either ‚Äúfundamentally correct‚Äù or ‚Äúobviously incorrect‚Äù. If three sections of a medical record are fundamentally accurate, then we consider the medical record to be correct. Overall, expert validation conclude that 94% of the records are deemed as correct.

4 Collaborative Diagnosis of LLMs Focused on Dispute Resolution

To further improve diagnostic accuracy, we propose a collaborative mechanism for clinical diagnosis. To streamline the process of forming a unified diagnostic report, we introduce the medical director to participate as a moderator. The overall process is shown in Figure 3 .

4.1 Exchange of Factual Information

We contend that a consensus on the physical condition of patients among intern doctors constitutes the cornerstone of collaborative diagnosis. The process of building the consensus is delineated into three distinct steps, outlined below.
(1) During the interactive consultation process, intern doctors communicate with the medical director, relaying patient factual information they have acquired, focusing primarily on symptoms and medical test outcomes.
(2) The medical director consolidates and analyzes the data collected from various intern doctors, confirming symptoms and test outcomes with patients and examiners to clarify disputed points.
(3) Drawing upon the findings received from intern doctors, coupled with feedback from patients and examiners, the medical director synthesizes a comprehensive summary of the patient‚Äôs symptoms and medical examination outcomes.

4.2 Discussions on Dispute Resolution

In collaborative diagnosis, the medical director should analyze the statements of intern doctors and identify key points of disagreement to foster focused discussions. The process is as follows.
(1) The collaborative diagnosis consists of multiple discussion iterations. Under the guidance of the medical director, intern doctors are expected to delve deeper gradually, resolve differences, and reach a consensus.
(2) In each session of collaborative diagnostic discussion, each intern doctor should present their diagnostic reports while engaging in critical analysis of their peers‚Äô findings. Guided by the medical director‚Äôs summary of disputed points among intern doctors, they can pinpoint the current issues requiring attention. This approach facilitates targeted and thorough critical thinking of intern doctors, enhancing the refinement of their reports.
(3) Upon the conclusion of discussions, the medical director assesses the persistence of disagreements among interns. If disagreements are identified, the director can summarize the controversial issues and set then as the agenda for the subsequent session to facilitate resolution. Conversely, if no disagreements are found, the director concludes the discussions and finalizes the diagnostic report by himself.

6 Analysis

6.1 Human Evaluation for Patient and Examiner Behavior

We analyze the behaviors of patients and examiners during interactive consultations, quantifying the interaction by tracking the number of question-and-answer (QA) pairs (conversation rounds) and the frequency of medical examination requests in each dialogue, denoted as #QA and #Test, respectively. Each consultation is assessed according to three primary metrics.
Relevance [QA] This metric evaluates the extent to which patients‚Äô responses are pertinent to the doctors‚Äô questions. It involves calculating the ratio of relevant answers provided by both patients and examiners to the total responses.
Consistency [QA] This involves assessing whether the responses of patients and examiners match with the information in the collected medical records.
Accuracy [Medical Examination] This measures the instruction-following of conducting medical examination, as we examine the frequency of examiners receive application and the rate at doctors inquiring about medical examinations.

We randomly select 50 dialogues from interactive consultations involving GPT-3.5, GPT-4, Wenxin-4.0, and Qwen-Max, resulting in total 200 instances for comprehensive evaluation. We manually annotate each utterance during the evaluation process. The results, presented in Table 4 , demonstrate high performance (over 98.0%) across all metrics of reliability with respect to patient and examiner interactions, regardless of the intern doctors powered by different LLMs. This highlights the efficacy of our interactive consultation system.

6.2 Efficiency of Dispute Resolution in Collaboration

For the ‚ÄúDispute Resolution‚Äù, we continue to check whether intern doctors can reach consensus more rapidly. In terms of efficiency, a comparative analysis is conducted on the number of discussion rounds necessary to achieve consensus, both with and without the ‚ÄúDispute Resolution‚Äù mechanism. The outcomes are detailed in Figure 4 . These findings reveal a marked increase in the rate of consensus achieved within the initial four discussion rounds following the adoption of the dispute resolution mechanism. This enhancement suggests that the process, facilitated by the medical director highlighting controversial issues and intern doctors concentrating on these discussions, effectively reduces the time required to achieve consensus.

6.4 Reasons for Failure Cases

We analyze cases for which GPT-4 provides incorrect diagnostic results and the medical director rates as 1 point. A total of 219 such cases are collected. By means of manual review, errors are categorized into three distinct types.
Omission of Auxiliary Examinations. An instance is the failure to detect gallbladder stones due to the absence of an abdominal ultrasound.
Exclusive Focus on Complications. For example, the attention of LLM is limited to soft tissue swelling in the feet, with concurrent neglect of the patient‚Äôs underlying diabetes.
Erroneous Judgment. While LLMs generally grasp the symptoms and the medical examination result, they make incorrect diagnosis, potentially reflecting inadequate medical expertise.

7 Related Works

Large Language Model in Medicine. Prior to the emergence of large language models (LLMs), the majority of automated diagnostic methods Zhong et¬†al. ( 2022 ); Chen et¬†al. ( 2023c ) relies on reinforcement learning to guide agents in gathering patient symptoms and conducting diagnoses. The development of LLMs in the medical sector has been driven by open-source Chinese LLMs and various fine-tuning methods. Models like Med-PaLM Singhal et¬†al. ( 2022 ) , CPLLM Shoham and Rappoport ( 2023 ) , DoctorGLM Xiong et¬†al. ( 2023 ) , BenTsao Wang et¬†al. ( 2023a ) , ChatGLM-Med Wang et¬†al. ( 2023b ) , Bianque-2 Chen et¬†al. ( 2023d ) , ChatMed-Consult Zhu and Wang ( 2023 ) , MedicalGPT Xu ( 2023 ) , and DISC-MedLLM Bao et¬†al. ( 2023 ) fine-tune using different datasets, techniques, and frameworks, focusing on medical question answering, health inquiries and doctor-patient dialogues.

Evaluation in Medicine. Prior research in medical evaluation has concentrated on non-interactive tasks, including question answering, entity and relation extraction, and medical summarization and generation. In the domain of biomedical question answering, key datasets such as MedQA (USMLE) Jin et¬†al. ( 2021 ) , PubMedQA Jin et¬†al. ( 2019 ) , and MedMCQA Pal et¬†al. ( 2022 ) are utilized, with accuracy serving as the primary evaluation metric. The objective of entity and relation extraction Li et¬†al. ( 2020 ) is to categorize named entities and their relationships from unstructured text into specific predefined classes. Prominent biomedical NER datasets include NCBI Disease Doƒüan et¬†al. ( 2014 ) , JNLPBA Collier and Kim ( 2004 ) , BC5CDR Li et¬†al. ( 2016 ) , BioRED Luo et¬†al. ( 2022 ) and IMCS Chen et¬†al. ( 2023b ) , with the F1 score being the standard for model performance assessment. Medical summarization and generation tasks involve converting structured data, like tables, into descriptive text. This includes the creation of patient clinic letters, radiology reports, and medical notes Liu et¬†al. ( 2023 ) . The principal datasets for these tasks are PubMed Jin et¬†al. ( 2019 ) and MentSum Sotudeh et¬†al. ( 2022 ) . A recent study introduced BioLeaflets Yermakov et¬†al. ( 2021 ) and assessed multiple Large Language Models (LLMs) in data-to-text generation.

LLM Powered Agents. The advancement of LLMs powered agents has led to significant strides in complex task resolution through human-like actions, such as role-playing and communication Xi et¬†al. ( 2023 ); Wang et¬†al. ( 2023c ) . This includes applications in software design and molecular dynamics simulation. Recent research Tang et¬†al. ( 2023 ) in the medical field has highlighted the critical roles and decision-making processes in medical QA, encompassing various investigations like CT scans, ultrasounds, electrocardiograms, and blood tests. Despite these advancements, effectively integrating LLM-based agents into the medical domain, particularly in disease diagnosis, presents a notable challenge Zhou et¬†al. ( 2023 ) . Our research pioneers the use of multi-agent systems in creating a clinical diagnosis environment. We introduce a novel mechanism for identifying, discussing, and resolving disputes in collaboration, demonstrating promising results in clinical diagnosis.

8 Conclusion

In AI Hospital, we take a step forward in the field of medical interactions by concentrating on clinical diagnosis. We introduce AI Hospital to build a real-time interactive consultation scenario. We generate simulated patients and examiner using collected medical records and established a comprehensive engineering process. Based on the platform, we build a benchmark MVME to explore the feasibility of different LLMs in interactive consultations. To improve the diagnostic accuracy, this research also introduces a novel collaborative mechanism for intern doctors, featuring iterative discussions and a dispute resolution process, supervised by a medical director. In our experiment, the results not only demonstrate the performance of different LLMs but also confirm the efficacy of our dispute resolution-centered collaborative approach. For in-depth analysis, we list the error types and identify the issues that should be addressed in the future.

Limitation

Insufficient Evaluation of Treatment Plan. In actual medical settings, treatment plans frequently vary; yet, our existing evaluation system merely gauges the alignment between the model‚Äôs proposed treatment and a standard reference plan. This system inadequately accounts for feasible alternative treatment strategies.
Risk of Environmental Impact. A significant risk associated with our AI Hospital is the potential escalation of environmental burdens stemming from the widespread utilization of OpenAI‚Äôs APIs for LLMs. This impact could be mitigated through the adoption of smaller-scale and open-source models that offer greater efficiency and reduced environmental impact in the future.

Ethics Consideration

Ethical considerations are paramount in our research. This section addresses the potential ethical implications of our study: Firstly, the medical records data utilized in our research, being publicly accessible online, will be released to enhance the reproducibility of our results. This enables other researchers to assess and extend our findings. Secondly, we emphasize privacy protection. The data sources have removed sensitive information prior to our collection, and we rigorously review our datasets to confirm the absence of sensitive details such as individual names.
