Abstract

StarCraft II is a challenging benchmark for AI agents due to the necessity of both precise micro-level operations and strategic macro-awareness. Previous works, such as Alphastar and SCC, achieve impressive performance on tackling StarCraft II , however, still exhibit deficiencies in long-term strategic planning and strategy interpretability. Emerging large language model (LLM) agents, such as Voyage and MetaGPT, presents the immense potential in solving intricate tasks. Motivated by this, we aim to validate the capabilities of LLMs on StarCraft II, a highly complex RTS game. To conveniently take full advantage of LLMs‚Äô reasoning abilities, we first develop textual StratCraft II environment, called TextStarCraft II, which LLM agent can interact. Secondly, we propose a Chain of Summarization method, including single-frame summarization for processing raw observations and multi-frame summarization for analyzing game information, providing command recommendations, and generating strategic decisions. Our experiment consists of two parts: first, an evaluation by human experts, which includes assessing the LLMs‚Äô mastery of StarCraft II knowledge and the performance of LLM agents in the game; second, the in-game performance of LLM agents, encompassing aspects like win rate and the impact of Chain of Summarization. Experiment results demonstrate that: 1. LLMs possess the relevant knowledge and complex planning abilities needed to address StarCraft II scenarios; 2. Human experts consider the performance of LLM agents to be close to that of an average player who has played StarCraft II for eight years; 3. LLM agents are capable of defeating the built-in AI at the Harder(Lv5) difficulty level. 1 Introduction

StarCraft II is one of the most popular and challenging esports games in the world today. With its high demands on players in strategic planning and real-time operations, StarCraft II has become an ideal testing platform for AI capabilities. DeepMind‚Äôs achievement in defeating world Go champion Lee Sedol with AlphaGo [ 19 ] [ 20 ] [ 18 ] . The BiCNet [ 16 ] marks a significant advancement in multi-agent coordination within StarCraft I AI research. StarCraft II emerged as the next conquest for AI. In 2019, DeepMind introduced the groundbreaking AlphaStar [ 22 ] , which defeated professional players TLO and MANA in public matches 3 3 3 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GKX6AcgFOZU , sparking increased attention from AI researchers. Subsequent releases, such as Mini-AlphaStar [ 12 ] , a simplified version with fewer network parameters and observation dimensions, and HierNet-SC2 [ 11 ] , capable of reinforcement learning without relying on supervised learning, further demonstrated the evolving landscape of AI in StarCraft II.Various AIs, such as SCC [ 26 ] (a Terran AI), and DI-Star 4 4 4 https://github.com/opendilab/DI-star (which defeated Chinese professional player Rex in an exhibition match and is deployable for home use), have showcased the advancements in AI development for StarCraft II. The rise of offline reinforcement learning led to the introduction of AlphaStar Unplugged [ 13 ] by DeepMind, which surpassed behavior clone AI trained in the original AlphaStar. Despite AI‚Äôs past successes against professional players, challenges emerged in traditional reinforcement learning for StarCraft II, including issues like lack of interpretability, short-sighted macro decisions, and slow unit transitions.

To address these challenges, we explore the potential of Chain of Thought (CoT) [ 28 ] in enhancing LLMs‚Äô reasoning abilities, potentially applying them to complex real-time strategic tasks.CoT has been extended to tree structures, resulting in CoT-SC [ 27 ] and Thought Trees (ToT) [ 30 ] , and even to graph structures like Graph of Thought (GoT) [ 2 ] . Recent works such as Voyager [ 24 ] , GITM [ 34 ] (Ghost in the Minecraft), and Spring [ 29 ] utilize language models as agents [ 25 ] , learning skills in complex open-world scenarios like Minecraft. In multi-agent tasks, works like Camel [ 9 ] and MetaGPT [ 8 ] employ multiple language model agents with specific roles to complete complex tasks such as brainstorming and software development [ 4 ] [ 17 ] . In the realm of social simulation, Generative Agents [ 15 ] innovatively use language model agents to create 25 different NPCs, simulating a human town to model human society.The introduction of CoT strengthens the reasoning and logical abilities of language models, enabling language model agents to demonstrate strategic interpretability and long-term planning advantages in natural language-described environments, potentially benefiting language model agents playing StarCraft II.

Therefore, this paper aims to leverage the strategic interpretability of language models and the logical reasoning capabilities of CoT to enable language model agents to engage in long-term strategic planning and real-time strategy adjustments in complex real-time strategy games like StarCraft II. To facilitate interaction between LLMs and the StarCraft II client, we propose TextStarCraft II, a text-based environment built on the python-sc2 framework. Using the python-sc2 5 5 5 https://github.com/BurnySc2/python-sc2 interface, we present raw game information in textual form, define language commands for common macro actions.For micro actions, we adopt a rule-based methodology akin to the one employed by OpenAI Five in Dota 2 [ 1 ] . This enables language models to engage in battles against StarCraft II‚Äôs built-in AI through TextStarCraft II.

Decision-making in StarCraft II involves handling complex information. It demands thorough, long-term planning and the ability to model opponents‚Äô strategies. All these tasks must be done while ensuring real-time responsiveness. However, the original Chain of Thought (CoT) method and its improvements encountered three main issues when applied to TextStarCraft II: 1. An inability to comprehend the complex and varied information of StarCraft II, 2. A failure to analyze the trends and game dynamics in StarCraft II situations, and 3. An inability to offer useful suggestions and decisions based on the current game state. To address these challenges, we introduce the Chain of Summarization(CoS) method, aimed at enhancing the LLM Agent‚Äôs capability to extract and analyze information for making comprehensive decisions. Our CoS method consists of two core modules: single-frame summarization and multi-frame summarization. The first, single-frame summarization, distills key information from raw data to help the LLM agent quickly grasp the current status of the game. The second, multi-frame summarization, processes several steps of critical information. This enables the LLM agent to swiftly analyze the ongoing game situation, offer counter-strategies, and make informed decisions.

In our experiments, we assessed various LLMs‚Äô knowledge of StarCraft II by using five part of question-and-answer challenges. Additionally, we compare the performance of different language models with the CoS approach, including GPT3.5-Turbo, GPT3.5-Turbo-16k, GPT4, Finetune-ChatGlm2 6b, and Finetune-Llama2 7b. Our findings show that under the CoS method, GPT3.5-Turbo-16k and GPT-4 demonstrate advanced reasoning. They successfully beat the StarCraft II Zerg AI at Harder (Level 5) difficulty playing as Protoss. This success showcases their ability to make complex strategic decisions, including scouting, expanding the economy, building defenses, upgrading research, and switching units. Finally, we conduct ablation experiments to validate the impact of the CoS method and different prompts on the performance of GPT3.5-Turbo-16k. Importantly, our experiments feature a robust evaluation system involving 30 grandmaster-level players and professional StarCraft II players who provide blind assessments of language models‚Äô knowledge mastery and strategic capabilities. 4 Chain of Summarization

In the intensely competitive and highly complex game of StarCraft II, making efficient and professional decisions is crucial. Existing Chains of Thought (CoT) methods and their derivatives perform well in simple tasks but still face challenges in complex decision-making and comprehensive analysis required in StarCraft II. These include difficulties in understanding the game‚Äôs situation, slow interaction speed, and less professional decision-making.To address these challenges, inspired by the cache mechanism of computer hardware and frame skipping techniques in reinforcement learning, we propose the Chain of Summarization method. We optimize the decision-making process through the following key strategies:

Information Compression: We summarize and abstract game data, eliminating unnecessary informational noise to extract the most critical game information.

Inference Acceleration: Abandoning traditional single-frame reasoning, we employ multi-frame information for a comprehensive analysis of the game situation and strategy formulation. This approach significantly reduces the time from analysis to action implementation.

Global Understanding: By compressing key information and using multi-frame analysis to discern game trends, language models can understand complex game strategies and dynamics, enabling professional decision-making and promoting higher-level strategic planning.

The Chain of Summarization method is capable of handling the intricacies and fast-paced strategies of StarCraft II, facilitating efficient and strategically significant decision-making.

Implementing the Chain of Summarization within game interactions involves four stages: initialization, single-frame and multi-frame summarization, directive formulation and action scheduling, and action retrieval and execution. The Chain of Summarization method excels in eliciting the macro-decision capabilities of the Large Language Model (LLM), enabling the generation of high-level, quality decisions from a plethora of game observations. 6 Discussion

In this study, we successfully introduced TextStarCraft II, a textual representation of the starcraft II environment. This representation enables the LLM agent to interact with the starcraft II game solely through natural language. Our novel multi-level summarization approach allows the language model to understand the game‚Äôs progression and discern strategies employed by both allies and adversaries. Coupled with our tailored prompt design, the language model can leverage historical human gameplay experience and recognize in-game scenarios in starcraft II, subsequently deciding upon the most suitable strategies to adopt.

Compared to previous works on the starcraft II full game, our methodology stands out by achieving competitive results without the necessity for additional data and training. Specifically, by solely utilizing the gpt3.5 API, our prompt design, and the multi-level summarization technique, we demonstrated zero-shot capability in defeating the medium-hard built-in AI. Furthermore, our LLM agent exemplifies higher-order decision-making capabilities absent in prior works. It excels in Adaptive Strategic Adjustment(Fig 6 .a), aligning its strategies in real-time with the opponents‚Äô troop transitions. The agent also demonstrates prowess in Technological Evolution and Innovation(Fig 5 .c), advancing in various tech domains proactively. It embodies Crisis Prevention and Management(Fig 5 .d) by constructing defensive structures preemptively, showcasing acute awareness of potential threats. Moreover, the agent‚Äôs Macro-economic and Defensive Strategizing(Fig 5 .c), coupled with its acumen in Intelligence Gathering and Analysis(Fig 5 .a and Fig 5 .b) illustrate its comprehensive strategic insight and versatile adaptability in StarCraftII‚Äôs intricate environments.

However, despite our achievements against several built-in AIs, we have identified several areas for improvement. Rule-Based Macro Actions: The current actions are heavily reliant on human prior knowledge as they are rule-based macro actions. We aim to mitigate this by incorporating hierarchical reinforcement learning in future implementations. Lack of Visual Information: The absence of visual input means the LLM agent lacks comprehensive battlefield awareness. Advancements in Visual Language Models (VLMs), such as miniGPT [ 33 ] , are believed to have the potential to address this limitation. No Feedback Loop: The existing environment does not provide a feedback loop, rendering the multi-level LLM agent incapable of learning. The incorporation of a feedback mechanism is in our plans for future research. Memory Limitations: Due to API call speed constraints, our model only achieves two-tiered summarization, leading to a lack of both short-term and long-term memory in our LLM agent. This is another area we intend to explore in our subsequent work.

We posit that, moving forward, TextStarCraft II will serve as an excellent benchmark for assessing the capabilities of both LLMs and LLM agents. Pitting LLM agents against each other on TextStarCraft II offers a more intuitive and intricate evaluation approach than traditional dataset assessments. Moreover, we envision a fusion of LLM and reinforcement learning in future starcraft II full game AIs. Such a combination can potentially lead to enhanced strategic gameplay and a heightened degree of interpretability, setting a new standard beyond what has been achieved with AlphaStar.
